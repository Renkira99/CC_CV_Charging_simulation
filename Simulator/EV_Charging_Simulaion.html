Dynamic Charging Theory

Key concepts from the literature review — understanding the physics behind the simulation

CC-CV Charging Method

The industry standard for Li-ion batteries. Constant Current (CC) phase charges from ~10-80% SoC with fixed high current while voltage rises. Constant Voltage (CV) phase caps voltage and lets current taper exponentially until the battery is full.

PCC = ICC × V(t) ↑
PCV = Vmax × I(t) ↓
🔄

Resonant Converter Topologies

Modern chargers use LLC, LCL, and LCC resonant converters that inherently provide CC-CV characteristics through the resonant tank behavior. Fixed-frequency operation enables predictable harmonic spectra and easier EMI filter design.

ηbest = 98.8% (2024)
ZVS + ZCS operation
〰️

Grid Harmonic Injection

Power electronic converters are non-linear loads that draw non-sinusoidal current. During CC mode, harmonic content is highest. The switching frequency and topology determine the harmonic spectrum. Under Indian standards (IEC 61000-3-2 adopted via IS 12360 and IS 17017 / AIS-138), THD is limited to 5% at the Point of Common Coupling (PCC) on India's 50 Hz grid.

THD = √(Σ Ih²) / I1 × 100%
🏗️

Distribution Grid Impact

Multiple EVs charging simultaneously with different SoC levels create a complex, time-varying load. Peak demand from CC-mode charging can overload distribution transformers. Coordinated charging can achieve 40.8% peak demand reduction.

Pagg(t) = Σ PEV,i(SoCi(t))
