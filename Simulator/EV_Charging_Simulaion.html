Dynamic Charging Characteristics of Electric Vehicles: An Interactive Harmonic and Load Analysis

First A. Author, Member, IEEE, Second B. Author, Fellow, IEEE
Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Technology
Email: author@institution.edu
Abstract—This simulation-based interactive article presents the dynamic charging characteristics of electric vehicles (EVs) using the constant-current constant-voltage (CC-CV) protocol. It investigates the harmonic distortion injected into the grid by common active and passive converter topologies. By adjusting parameters such as battery capacity, target SoC, and charging levels, the dynamic total harmonic distortion (THD) and spectrum compliance against IEEE 519 and IEC 61000-3-2 standards can be observed in real time.

I. Introduction

The rapid proliferation of electric vehicles (EVs) presents significant challenges to the power distribution grid. Because EV chargers utilize non-linear power electronic converters, they draw distorted grid currents, negatively impacting power quality [1]. Furthermore, modern lithium-ion batteries are exclusively charged using the constant-current constant-voltage (CC-CV) protocol. This leads to dynamic grid loading where the active power demand and harmonic injection vary significantly as the State of Charge (SoC) increases.

This article provides a live, interactive execution of the charging models. The user may adjust the battery pack constraints and converter characteristics in Section II to immediately observe the impact on grid harmonics simulated in Section III.

III. Simulation Results

The time-domain characteristics of the charging session are obtained by discrete-time integration. The transition from Constant Current (CC) to Constant Voltage (CV) typically incurs a reduction in overall power transfer resulting in varying grid harmonic properties.

V. Conclusion

This web-based dynamic simulator effectively models the electrical characteristics of a commercial EV pack interfacing with non-linear rectifying systems. It demonstrates the profound interaction between battery State of Charge and grid-side harmonic signatures, affirming the importance of topology selection in modern charging infrastructures.
